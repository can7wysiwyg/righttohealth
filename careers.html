At Right to Health (RTH), we recognize that our most valuable asset is our dedicated team of professionals who share our unwavering commitment to advancing health equity in Malawi.

We believe that individuals with passion, expertise, and a desire to make a meaningful impact are the driving force behind our success.
That's why we offer exceptional career opportunities for those who wish to join us in our mission.
What Makes a Career at RTH Unique?
  • Purpose-Driven Work : When you join RTH, you become part of an organization that is not just about jobs but about a higher purpose. Our work directly impacts the lives of hard-to-reach, vulnerable, and neglected populations in Malawi. You will wake up every day knowing that your efforts are contributing to improving health outcomes and reducing disparities.
  • Health Equity Advocacy : RTH is more than a workplace; it's a platform for advocacy. We actively engage in advocating for policies and practices that promote health equity. As a member of our team, you will have the opportunity to amplify your voice and contribute to meaningful change.
  • Collaborative Environment : Our organization thrives on collaboration. We believe in the power of teamwork and partnerships. You will have the chance to work with colleagues who are passionate, diverse, and committed to achieving common goals.
  • Professional Growth : At RTH, we invest in the growth and development of our team members. Whether it's through training, mentorship, or exposure to challenging projects, we provide the resources and support you need to advance in your career.
  • Community Engagement : We don't just work for communities; we work with them. RTH actively engages with local populations, and our team members often have the opportunity to interact directly with beneficiaries, gaining valuable insights into their needs and aspirations.

Volunteer Opportunities

Be the Change You Want to See -

When you volunteer at Right to Health (RTH), you're not just donating your time; you're investing in a healthier, more equitable Malawi. Our volunteers play a vital role in our mission to address health disparities and improve access to healthcare services for vulnerable populations. Here's why volunteering at RTH is an opportunity like no other:

  • Be a Changemaker
    - As a volunteer at RTH, you become an active part of the solution. You have the chance to directly impact the lives of those who need it most, making a tangible difference in healthcare outcomes.
  • Learn and Grow
    - Volunteering is a continuous learning experience. You'll work alongside dedicated professionals, gaining valuable insights into public health, healthcare delivery, and health equity. It's an opportunity to enhance your skills and knowledge.
  • Community Engagement
    - RTH believes in community-led solutions. As a volunteer, you'll have the opportunity to engage with local communities, empowering individuals to take control of their health and well-being. You'll experience the richness of Malawi's diverse cultures.
  • Diverse Opportunities
    - We offer a range of volunteer opportunities in areas such as healthcare delivery, advocacy, research, community outreach, and more. You can choose a role that aligns with your interests and career goals
  • Networking and Collaboration
    - RTH is a hub of professionals committed to healthcare and social justice. Volunteering opens doors to meaningful connections with experts in the field, potentially shaping your future career.
  • Volunteer Opportunities at RTH
    -Our volunteer opportunities are diverse and designed to cater to various interests and skill sets. Whether you're a healthcare professional, researcher, advocate, or community enthusiast, there's a place for you at RTH. Some of our volunteer roles include:
  • Healthcare Delivery Volunteers
    - Assist in clinics, outreach programs, and health education initiatives, directly impacting the health of communities.
  • Advocacy Volunteers
    - Join our advocacy efforts by working on policy analysis, research, and campaigns focused on health equity.
  • Research and Evaluation Volunteers
    - Contribute to evidence-based decision-making by assisting in research projects, data analysis, and program evaluation.
  • Community Engagement Volunteers
    - Engage with local communities, fostering community-led healthcare initiatives and promoting health awareness.
