Our Work

Right to Health (RTH) can offer a range of health programs in Malawi to address the specific health needs of the population.

The provision of primary healthcare services through both static and outreach/mobile clinics is a comprehensive approach to ensure that healthcare reaches every person, especially those in remote or underserved areas.

These programs can be designed to promote health equity, improve access to healthcare services, and tackle the root causes of health disparities including:

Maternal and Child Health Program
  • Providing prenatal care and education to expectant mothers.
  • Ensuring safe deliveries and access to skilled birth attendants.
  • Offering postnatal care and support for newborns and mothers.
Immunization and Preventive Care
  • Organizing vaccination campaigns to increase immunization coverage.
  • Promoting preventive measures such as mosquito net distribution to combat malaria.
Nutrition and Food Security Program
  • Addressing malnutrition through nutrition education and supplementary feeding programs.
  • Supporting community gardens and agricultural initiatives to enhance food security. Supporting community gardens and agricultural initiatives to enhance food security.
Infectious Disease Control
  • Developing strategies to combat prevalent diseases like HIV/AIDS, tuberculosis, and malaria.
  • Promoting safe practices and awareness to reduce the spread of infectious diseases.
Health Education and Awareness
  • Conducting health education campaigns on topics like family planning, hygiene, and sanitation.
  • Empowering communities with knowledge to make informed health decisions.
Access to Clean Water and Sanitation
  • Supporting projects that provide access to clean drinking water and improved sanitation facilities.
  • Reducing waterborne diseases through infrastructure development.
Mental Health and Psychosocial Support
  • Raising awareness about mental health issues and reducing stigma.
  • Offering counseling and support services for individuals facing mental health challenges.
Strengthening the Capacity of Community Health Worker
  • Training and deploying community health workers to reach remote and underserved areas.
  • Building local capacity for basic healthcare and health education.
Health Infrastructure Development
  • Supporting the construction and maintenance of health clinics and facilities in underserved regions.
  • Ensuring essential medical equipment and supplies are available.
Health Equity Advocacy
  • Advocating for policies and resources that prioritize health equity and address social determinants of health.
  • Collaborating with local and national authorities to improve healthcare access.
Health Data Collection and Analysis
  • Gathering and analyzing health data to identify trends and inform program planning.
  • Using evidence-based approaches to guide interventions.
Emergency Response and Preparedness
  • Developing emergency response plans and providing assistance during health crises.
  • Ensuring communities are prepared for disasters and health emergencies.
